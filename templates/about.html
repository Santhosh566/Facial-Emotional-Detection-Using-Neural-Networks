Facial Emotional Detection Using Neural Networks

Facial Emotional Detection Using Neural Networks leverages a dataset sourced from Kaggle, a popular platform for machine learning datasets. This dataset is organized into training and validation folders, each containing seven directories corresponding to different emotions: angry, happy, sad, surprise, neutral, fear, and disgust. The training data, spread across these directories, enables the CNN model to learn patterns and features associated with each emotion, contributing to an overall accuracy of 70%. The data preprocessing phase involves standardizing image sizes, enhancing quality, and extracting key facial features. These steps are crucial for the model to effectively distinguish and classify emotions from facial expressions. During training, the CNN model learns to recognize spatial hierarchies of features, optimizing its ability to accurately identify emotions in real-time facial images or video streams. Hyperparameter tuning and optimization techniques further refine the model's performance, enhancing its accuracy in emotion recognition tasks.
